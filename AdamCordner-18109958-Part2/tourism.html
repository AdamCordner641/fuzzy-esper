Find out how you can visit Brougham Castle

In the current day, Brougham Castle is maintained by the English Heritage and is fully available for public viewing. For directions to the site simply enter the following address: 'Moor Lane , Penrith, Cumbria' or postcode: 'CA10 2AA' into your sat nav, or visit the English Heritage website for more information about bus and train access. No on site parking is available to visitors, however there is free roadside parking nearby.

For customers concerned about food and drinks there is an on site shop which serves tea, coffee and hot chocolate. Cold drinks and various snacks are also available. Additionally, ice cream is sold in the summer months. Near the site's entrance is the gift shop. This shop sells a variety of Brougham Castle souvenirs and gifts, including books and toys.
Unisex and separate disabled toilets are available to guests, however, no baby changing facilities. You are welcome to bring dogs as long as they are kept on a leads. Please note, however, that they are not allowed on the private land by the river.
Within the main shop is an exhibition highlighting the incredible and remarkable life of Lady Anne Clifford, one of the most noteworthy inheritors of Brougham Castle. The exhibition also showcases some stone carvings and pottery finds from the Roman fort. The castle itself is a complex maze of passages and spiral staircases, making it a fascinating and fun place to explore. Climb to the top of the Keep for a stunning panoramic view over the Eden Valley and the earthworks of the adjacent Roman fort of Brovacum.
